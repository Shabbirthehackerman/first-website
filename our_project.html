Properties of Different Pipe Materials

  • Plastic

  • Pros: lightweight; resitance to chemicals and abrasion
  • Cons: low tensile strenght; largely affected by temperature

  • Steel

  • Pros: easily extended for long distances; can be of large diameter
  • Cons: cannot be used underground without proper coating; shorter lifespan

  • Cast Iron and Ductile Iron

  • Pros: much less expensive than steel; very strong for its weight
  • Cons: corrodes easily; very heavy

  • Concrete

  • Pros: best for pipelines of very large diameter; useful for transporting water
  • Cons: easily corroded

Different Mediums for Pipeline Transportation

  • Electromagnetic/Electric

    • Pros: very quiet; very precise (an accuracy of ± 0.000315 in.); transports solids up to a rate of 1080kph
    • Cons: requires compicated electrical wiring; requires acceleration and deceleration process; high cost

  • Pneumatic

    • Pros: no acceleration and deceleration process required; simple, no complicated wiring; not too slow (22-55kph)
    • Cons: not very energy efficent; cannot propel solids for long distances (more than a mile);

  • Hydraulic

    • Pros: can decerlate and accelerate quickly; steady speed; efficent; high torque
    • Cons: steady but very slow (7 - 11kph); pipes may leak
